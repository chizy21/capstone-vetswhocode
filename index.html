About Me

Hello there, I am a Navy veteran, having served from 1985-1989. I started out as a Boatswains Mate (deckhand), and after failing down during one of my deployments, I was moved into Supply as a Storekeeper.

After the Navy, I went on to work in the private sector as a Deputy Sheriff until my service-connected disability got to the point I could no longer serve. I decided to get into the computer field in desktop support.

As a self taught IT guy, I moved up to helpdesk support, then landed a job at Compaq Server Division supporting Windows, Linux, Novell installations

After a few years there, I then landed a job at the VA Hospital in Houston, TX in Network Admin. Here I setup and deployed Cisco routers and switches.

How I ended up learning web development

From the days of AOL and MySpace, I self taught how to make simple websites using HTML only. I am a member of the DAV, VFW, American Legion, BVA, BVA-Bits. In these veteran service membership organizations, I have found that they all need websites to reach out to the younger generation of veterans. That is what led me to search fro some type of web development courses and I enrolled in Devslopes hoping that would work. But they are mostly you watch a video and then code along, not having any realtime way to ask questions outside of posting on their discord server.
